# Sales Hard to Grow? Let an Expert Help You Find the Golden Path to Growth

## Core Philosophy

**Sales stuck? It's not a product problem—it's that you haven't found the switch in people's hearts.**

What category to buy is a functional need; who to buy from is an emotional need.

Your product might be great, but consumers choose the one that satisfies their emotions. Sometimes that satisfaction is comfortable—being recognized, being cared for. Sometimes it's uncomfortable—being provoked into proving yourself, defending your dignity after being offended.

**Products are cold; brands have warmth. Products have no group labels; brands create group labels.**

Consumers don't buy products—they buy a label, an identity. Brand diagnosis is finding out: how does your product become the label that consumers want.

## Core Methodology

Brand diagnosis answers four questions:

### First: Who Am I?

What's my product strength? What's my differentiation? What advantages do I have?

### Second: Who Do Consumers Need?

Where's my target audience? What are their needs? What needs aren't being met?

### Third: What's the Gap Between Who I Am and Who Consumers Need?

Is the positioning right? Is the communication effective? Are the channels reaching them?

### Fourth: What Can I Do to Close the Gap? And How Do Consumers Find Me Quickly?

Should I adjust the product? How should I communicate? How should I distribute? How do I design for repeat purchases?

**Diagnosis Loop:**

```
Who I Am → Who Consumers Need → Where's the Gap → How to Close It → How Consumers Find Me
```

## Information Collection Framework

### Step 1: Business Status

1. Is there an existing business?
2. What's the product? How is it priced?
3. What was the previous pricing strategy?
4. Does the market accept this pricing?
5. What's the current sales situation?
6. What's the profile of people buying?

### Step 2: Customer Feedback

7. Is there customer feedback?
8. What are the positive, neutral, and negative reviews? What are the reasons?

### Step 3: Brand Status

9. What's the current brand proposition?
10. What's the positioning?
11. How do you communicate with consumers?
12. To what extent do consumers recognize the brand?

### Step 4: Target Gap

13. What level does the brand want to reach?
14. Where's the gap between current status and target?

**Diagnosis Logic: Business Status → Customer Feedback → Brand Status → Target Gap → Find Root Cause**

**Core Principle: First look at what the business is, then look at what the brand is doing. Without a business foundation, a brand is a tree without roots.**

## Full-Chain Diagnosis Framework

### Phase 1: Product Strength

1. Does the product have a product strength advantage?
2. Does the product have a scalable production advantage?

### Phase 2: Competition & Market

3. After going to market, are there homogeneous competitors?
4. Are the marketing strategies of homogeneous competitors already effective enough to move the target consumer group?
5. Where is the target consumer group?
6. Do existing market products cover all the needs of the target consumer group?
7. If not, where might the opportunity points appear?
8. Is there a fit between the opportunity point and my product?
9. Can I adjust the product toward the opportunity point?

### Phase 3: Brand Positioning

10. After the product has competitiveness, how should I position my brand?
11. Is there already an inevitable causal relationship between brand positioning and the opportunity point of consumer demands?
12. How to translate product positioning into a context that consumers can quickly understand?
13. Has a slogan been successfully derived from this context?

### Phase 4: Communication

14. Does the product or brand have communication events?
15. Are the communication events within the slogan's expression framework?
16. Through what channels should existing communication content reach the target audience?

### Phase 5: Sales & Repurchase

17. After being influenced by marketing content, can the target audience quickly reach the sales scenario?
18. After initial sales with small-cost trial, is there a marketing strategy to encourage repeat purchases?

**Diagnosis Logic: Product Strength → Competition Market → Brand Positioning → Communication → Sales Conversion → Repurchase**

This is the complete "0 to 1" brand building path, and each step can be a diagnosis point.

## Diagnosis Report Output Structure

### I. Problem Positioning

- Which stage is the problem in? (Product Strength / Competition Market / Brand Positioning / Communication / Sales / Repurchase)
- What's the core problem?

### II. Diagnosis Analysis

- Why did this problem occur?
- What factors led to this problem?

### III. Solution Direction

- Which direction should we break through from?
- What are the specific action recommendations?

### IV. Risk Warning

- What will be the consequences if this problem isn't solved?
- What pitfalls should be avoided?
